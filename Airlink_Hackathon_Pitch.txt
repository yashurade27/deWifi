Opening Hook

"Pure software is rapidly becoming un-investable."

Any software you can imagine can now be built by a small-city startup in a weeks using AI. Investing in software-only plays is starting to feel pointless.

But here's the edge case: hardware, physical infrastructure, and location-bound services cannot be vibe-coded.

People still buy and trust branded routers. They trust physical access points. They trust on-site infrastructure. That's precisely why our approach is different.

Airlink is not just another app. It's a software + hardware-enabled marketplace that leverages physical devices and location-bound connectivity — something no AI can replicate overnight.

---

The Problem

Picture this: You land in a new city. Your phone is on 2G. The hotel wants ₹800 for WiFi. The café WiFi drops every 10 minutes. And the guy in the apartment upstairs has a 200 Mbps fiber connection sitting at 20% utilization — all day.

This is the daily reality for millions of travelers, students, gig workers, and remote professionals across India.

- Hotel WiFi: ₹500–1,000/day — expensive, slow, unreliable
- Public WiFi: insecure, congested, unusable
- Mobile data: ₹2.5/GB — fine outdoors, dead inside buildings

Meanwhile, 70% of home internet bandwidth goes completely unused during the day.

We have a supply problem and a demand problem sitting right next to each other — and no one is connecting them.

---

Root Cause

Two things are broken:

1. Information asymmetry — People with spare bandwidth and people who need short-term access have no way to find or trust each other.
2. Trust & access control — Sharing a WiFi password is a security nightmare. There's no safe, simple way to grant temporary, paid, revokable access.

No existing platform solves both. Until now.

---

The Solution: Airlink

"Airbnb for WiFi."

Airlink is a peer-to-peer WiFi sharing marketplace built by Team QuadCoders. We connect two groups of people:

WiFi Owners — homeowners, café operators, offices with spare bandwidth — who can list their connection, set a price per hour, and earn passive income while they sleep.

WiFi Seekers — travelers, students, delivery partners, remote workers — who can open the app, find nearby spots on a live map, see real speeds and reviews, book instantly, and connect securely.

No password sharing. No monthly subscriptions. Pay for exactly the bandwidth you use, for exactly as long as you need it.

And crucially — access is controlled through a captive portal + token-based system backed by hardware-level enforcement. Not a PIN. Not a shared password. A secure, time-limited session that expires automatically when the booking ends.

We're not building a new ISP. We're building the marketplace layer on top of existing infrastructure — turning idle bandwidth into an accessible resource for everyone.

The Hardware Moat

This is where we're genuinely different from every other platform in this space.

Anyone can clone our app UI in a weekend. But our gateway layer — the captive portal running on physical hardware at the host's location — creates a real-world moat. It enforces access at the network level, integrates with payment verification, and issues time-limited tokens. It's physical infrastructure. It cannot be forked.

We're building Airbnb, not just a listings website. The hardware is our lock-in.
